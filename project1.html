iRobot (Mars Rover)

It introduced me to embedded programming, which I enjoyed so much I decided to pursue an emphasis in. The class began with a fairly in-depth intro to C programming. This was easy, given that I had already taken classes in C and used C in industry at this point in my career. The new material came as we got into embedded topics such as bitwise operations for faster multiplication and division, interrupts, pulse-width modulation, and UART. I found these topics fascinating and thoroughly enjoyed the accompanying labs.

The project consist of iRobot to traverse a course of obstacles starting at a random “landing point” to a retrieval zone within a set amount of time. This all needs to be done without bumping into tall objects, without falling into holes in the floor, without leaving the course bounded by IR beams, and without having to visually confirm where the robot is on the obstacle course.
